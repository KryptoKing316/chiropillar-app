## 00 · A note on language — Why the words matter

> The world's vocabulary encodes the world's extraction.

The acquisition market runs on words that carry a worldview: *acquire, loan, underwrite, foreclose, exit, buy out, leverage*. Each one assumes a person's life's work is a commodity to be bought, levered, and resold — and that whoever takes it over does so as a debtor.

Where this document describes the worldly stack, it uses the world's words, because you need to recognize what we replace. Where it describes what Firma does, it uses our words: **steward, provision, ownership earned, generosity, flourishing**. Not euphemism — precision. The mechanic is genuinely different, so the words are too.

**Abundance Over Extraction** is one of our five immutable principles. We cannot carry debt in the ecosystem. Not a preference — a law.

## 01 · The model in one paragraph — What this is

> A non-debt acquisition that hands ownership to the people who carry it.

The worldly playbook for buying a business is leverage: a buyer borrows against the business, carries personal liability for a decade, and services debt before they earn a dollar of real ownership. We replace that entirely. Our **Sovereign Wealth Fund** buys the business outright — the retiring owner is paid in full at close and walks away whole. Then, instead of saddling the next person with a loan, we extend the business to whoever wants to **steward** it — and from day one we allocate them a portion of ownership simply because they're carrying it.

From there, ownership is **earned**, not repaid. The more the steward works, the more they get the business going, the more they participate — and the more generous they are — the faster they earn additional stewardship. More stewardship means more revenue and more economic rights. And if it doesn't work out, **they owe us nothing**. There is no debt to collect. We simply own a business, and we find the next steward on 1Accord. The risk is ours. The upside is theirs.

> Supporting a business helps it flourish. The business flourishing helps the stewards. The community helping accelerates everyone. The better everything gets, the more impact flows back to the community.

## 02 · Why it's different — Debt vs. stewardship acquisition

> Same business changing hands. Opposite architecture underneath.

| The worldly debt acquisition | The Firma stewardship acquisition |
|---|---|
| Buyer borrows against the business (SBA loan, leverage) | The Sovereign Wealth Fund buys it outright, debt-free |
| Seller often carries paper or waits on buyer financing | Seller is paid in full at close — no financing contingency to collapse the deal |
| New owner starts owing everything, owns nothing | Steward starts already holding a portion, just for carrying it |
| Ownership earned by paying down a note | Ownership earned by stewarding it well — work, contribution, generosity |
| If it fails, the owner is buried in personal debt | If it doesn't work out, the steward owes nothing — the risk was ours |
| Business is leveraged, often stripped for returns | Business is supported — compute, energy, agentic labor, community backing |
| One owner extracts; the team are employees | The team can hold fractional stewardship; the community can too |

Every row on the left has an extraction point built in — the debt, the leverage, the strip. The right side has none. That's not a softer version of the same deal. It's a different instrument.

From day one we allocate them a portion of stewardship — just for carrying it. They don't start at zero. They start as a part-owner, and everything from here grows that share.

## 04 · How ownership is earned — Earned, not repaid

> You don't pay us back with money. You earn ownership with stewardship.

In a debt deal, the only way to earn ownership is to pay down the loan with cash. Here, ownership grows through everything the steward puts in — and money is only one input. The more of these flow, the faster stewardship transfers:

- **Revenue** — A portion of the business's revenue flows back toward the Fund's position — not as a debt payment, but as the business earning its way into the steward's hands.
- **Compute** — The Realm connected to the business contributes compute to the substrate. Productive contribution counts toward ownership — the business that gives more, earns more.
- **Energy** — Surplus energy through Photon flows back to the ecosystem and counts the same way. The business that powers others moves faster toward owning itself.
- **Reputation** — The steward's reputation — reliability, delivery, how they treat their people and customers — weights how fast ownership accrues. Character is an input, not an afterthought.
- **Generosity** — Generosity accelerates everything, and it's scaled proportionally — a steward who shares a meaningful slice outpaces one who hoards a large one. If they choose to give a percentage back to the community, that earns economic rights and speeds the whole track.
- **Economic rights · bonding curves** — As the steward earns, their economic rights in the business accrue along a bonding curve — the position deepens as the business grows and as they contribute. More stewardship means more revenue to them and more economic rights. The Fund's position is steadily outweighed as theirs grows — never collected, never foreclosed, simply outpaced by the value they create.

> If they aren't successful, they owe us nothing. We own a business, and we find the next steward on 1Accord. If they are successful, they walk a clean road to owning it outright.

## 05 · Who shares in it — Four participants, one direction

> Everyone who pours in, holds a piece.

**01 · Honored — The retiring owner**
Eric's client. Paid in full at close, walks away whole, legacy intact. May choose to keep a stake in the business they built — or take clean proceeds and be done. Their choice.

**02 · Earning — The steward**
The human CEO, agentic team, or hybrid matched on 1Accord. Starts with an allocated portion, earns toward full ownership through revenue, contribution, reputation, and generosity. Can eventually buy the whole thing — or keep it shared.

**03 · Stewarding — The employees**
The team that runs the business day to day can hold fractional stewardship — not staff waiting on a paycheck, but part-owners of what they help flourish. The owner-independence that made the business valuable becomes shared ownership.

**04 · Backing — The community**
Anyone in the ecosystem can support a business they believe in — sow into it, hold a fractional position. Community support gives the business more capital, which speeds the steward's path — and rewards the community as it flourishes.

And underneath all four: our **Sovereign Wealth Fund**, which bought the business, carries the risk, and holds the residual position that gets steadily outweighed as everyone else earns. The Fund's recovered position recycles into the next acquisition. The model funds its own growth.

## 06 · The flourishing flywheel — No extraction point anywhere

> Everyone's flourishing is upstream of everyone else's.

1. The Fund and community support the business.
2. Supported, the business flourishes — more revenue, more capital, lighter operations.
3. A flourishing business moves the steward and employees faster toward ownership.
4. Owners who flourish are generous — sharing back to the community.
5. A rewarded community has more to support the next business.

In a debt acquisition, the participants are adversaries: the lender's gain is the borrower's burden, the buyer's return comes out of the business's hide. Here there are no adversaries. The Fund's recovery, the steward's ownership, the employees' stake, the community's dividend, and the business's health all move the same direction — or none of them move.

This is what **Abundance Over Extraction** looks like when it stops being a principle on a page and becomes a working financial instrument. **A flywheel with no extraction point anywhere in it.**

## 08 · The open door — Full ownership, or stay — their choice

> No balloon payment. No maturity date. Just a door that opens when they're ready.

As the steward earns, they reach a point where they can take full ownership if they want — buy the whole thing outright at a clean formulaic value — or simply keep it where it is and continue in the shared arrangement, collecting their share while the community and Fund keep theirs.

There is no forced exit. No clock running out. No moment where a payment comes due and the whole thing is at risk. The steward owns more and more over time, and the door to full ownership stays open — to walk through whenever they choose, or never, as they prefer. **A debt deal ends in a reckoning. This ends in a choice.**

> A debt acquisition asks: can you pay it off before it crushes you? The stewardship acquisition asks: how much do you want to own, and when?

> We don't lend against the business. We buy it, support it, and hand it to whoever will steward it.
> Owner paid whole · Steward owes nothing · Everyone who pours in, holds a piece.
